Understanding the condition

What actually happens
when you have Hepatitis?

Hepatitis is inflammation of the liver — one of the most vital organs in the human body. The liver processes everything you eat, drink, and absorb, filtering toxins from the blood, producing bile for digestion, and manufacturing essential proteins. When the liver is inflamed and damaged, everything breaks down.

Hepatitis A, B and C are caused by three different viruses, but all three result in the same outcome — liver inflammation, cell damage, and if left untreated, progressive scarring (cirrhosis) or liver failure. The key is healing the liver tissue itself, not just suppressing the virus.

1

The virus attacks liver cells (hepatocytes)

Hepatitis viruses enter the body and target liver cells directly, hijacking them to replicate and spreading the infection through the liver tissue.

2

The immune system fights back — causing inflammation

The immune system attacks infected cells, causing the liver to swell and inflame. This inflammation — while protective — also damages surrounding healthy liver tissue.

3

Liver function begins to fail

Damaged cells cannot filter toxins, produce bile, or regulate blood proteins. Jaundice, fatigue, nausea, and dark urine appear as the liver struggles to function.

4

Chronic infection leads to scarring (cirrhosis)

In Hepatitis B and C especially, chronic infection causes scar tissue to replace healthy liver cells over years. Unchecked, this progresses to cirrhosis or liver cancer.
